This Acceptable Use Policy ("**AUP**") governs your use of the Gigaton platform, including the website, application, API, AI features, Connectors, and any related products and services (collectively, the "**Service**"). This AUP is incorporated by reference into the [Terms of Service](terms_of_service.md). Capitalized terms not defined here have the meanings given in the [Terms of Service](terms_of_service.md).

Violation of this AUP is a material breach of the Terms of Service and may result in suspension or termination of your Account, deletion of offending content, and/or referral to law enforcement.

## 1. Purpose

The Service is designed to facilitate predictably profitable interaction management for legitimate businesses. We require that you use the Service in a manner that is **lawful, safe, respectful of others, and consistent with the integrity of the Service and the broader internet ecosystem.**

## 2. Prohibited Uses

You may not use the Service, or allow any Authorized User or third party to use the Service, to:

### 2.1 Illegal activity

(a) Violate any applicable U.S., Mexican, state, provincial, or local law, regulation, treaty, or court order.
(b) Engage in or facilitate fraud, money laundering, tax evasion, sanctions evasion, or terrorism financing.
(c) Infringe the intellectual property rights, privacy rights, publicity rights, or other legal rights of any person.
(d) Distribute or facilitate the distribution of child sexual abuse material (CSAM), non-consensual intimate imagery, or content depicting violence against minors.
(e) Engage in human trafficking, exploitation of minors, or any conduct prohibited by 18 U.S.C. § 2252A or analogous statutes.

### 2.2 Spam and unsolicited communications

(a) Send unsolicited commercial messages ("spam") via any channel routed through the Service (email, SMS, WhatsApp, web push) in violation of CAN-SPAM, TCPA, the Mexican LFPDPPP, the FTC Telemarketing Sales Rule, or any equivalent law.
(b) Send bulk messages to recipients who have not opted in or have opted out.
(c) Use harvested, scraped, or purchased contact lists without verifiable consent.
(d) Falsify the sender, subject line, or routing path of messages.
(e) Operate the Service in a manner that triggers spam complaints exceeding industry benchmarks set by Twilio, Meta (WhatsApp), Google, or other Sub-processors. Excessive complaints may result in suspension regardless of the underlying intent.

### 2.3 Scraping and unauthorized data collection

(a) Use the Service to scrape, harvest, crawl, or extract data from any website, application, or service in violation of that party's terms of service or applicable law.
(b) Circumvent any technical access controls (CAPTCHAs, rate limits, robots.txt directives, login walls) on third-party sites or APIs accessed through Connectors.
(c) Build or maintain databases of personal information without a lawful basis.
(d) Use the Service to clone, mirror, or republish third-party content without permission.

### 2.4 Abusive, harmful, or harassing content

(a) Use the Service to generate, store, or distribute content that is harassing, threatening, defamatory, obscene, hateful, or that incites violence against any person or group.
(b) Use AI features to generate impersonations of real persons without their consent, including deepfakes, voice clones, or synthetic identities, except where (i) the subject has clearly consented in writing, (ii) the content is clearly labeled as synthetic, and (iii) the use does not deceive a viewer into believing the subject said or did something they did not.
(c) Use the Service to facilitate harassment campaigns, doxxing, swatting, or coordinated abuse.
(d) Use the Service to deny, distort, or trivialize atrocities or historical violence.

### 2.5 Credential stuffing, account abuse, and authentication attacks

(a) Use the Service to test stolen, leaked, or purchased credentials against any login system.
(b) Operate password-cracking, brute-force, dictionary, or credential-stuffing tools through the Service.
(c) Create or maintain accounts on third-party services using identities you are not authorized to use.
(d) Bypass, attempt to bypass, or assist others in bypassing the Service's authentication, sign-off matrix, multi-tenant isolation, or rate-limiting mechanisms.
(e) Share your credentials with anyone outside your authorized organization, or use another person's credentials without their authorization.

### 2.6 Service interference and security violations

(a) Probe, scan, or test the vulnerability of the Service or any related network without prior written authorization from `security@gigaton.ai` (TODO: confirm).
(b) Interfere with or disrupt the Service, including by introducing malware, ransomware, viruses, Trojans, worms, or other malicious code; by conducting denial-of-service (DoS) or distributed-denial-of-service (DDoS) attacks; or by exceeding documented rate limits.
(c) Reverse engineer, decompile, or disassemble any portion of the Service.
(d) Attempt to gain unauthorized access to any account, computer system, or network connected to the Service.
(e) Use the Service to attack, threaten, or attempt to compromise other services, networks, or users.

### 2.7 AI misuse

(a) Use AI features to generate content that violates Section 2.4.
(b) Use AI features to create disinformation, misleading political content, fraudulent product or service claims, or false reviews/endorsements.
(c) Use AI features to generate legal, medical, tax, financial, or psychotherapy advice held out as professional advice without appropriate professional oversight and disclaimers.
(d) Use AI features to mass-generate low-quality content for SEO manipulation, link farms, or content farms.
(e) Use AI features to circumvent content-moderation systems on any platform, including by laundering prohibited content through generated paraphrases.
(f) Use AI features to develop CBRN weapons (chemical, biological, radiological, nuclear), malware, or other tools designed to cause significant harm.
