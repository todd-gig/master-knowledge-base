## Prompt:
explain how to craft a vision statement

## Response:
A vision statement defines the **future state you want to create**. It’s a concise, inspiring description of what success looks like long term. The purpose is to guide all strategic and operational decisions by anchoring them in a shared, emotionally compelling future.

**Steps to craft one:**

1.  **Start with “Why.”**  
    Identify your core purpose — why your organization or initiative exists beyond profit. Ask:
    *   “What would the world lose if we didn’t exist?”
    *   “What change do we want to see in the world?”
2.  **Describe the ideal future.**  
    Paint a picture of what success looks and feels like 10–20 years from now. It must be **aspirational but attainable**.  
    Example prompts:
    *   “If we achieve our mission, what will the world look like?”
    *   “What impact will we have had on people or systems?”
3.  **Make it human-centered.**  
    Describe transformation or benefit for people, not just operational milestones. It should express **human flourishing, empowerment, or progress**.
4.  **Be concise and memorable.**  
    10–20 words is ideal. Every word must earn its place. Avoid jargon. Use active, present-tense language.
5.  **Ensure alignment with your “Why.”**  
    The vision must logically emerge from your core belief or purpose.  
    Formula:
    > _“A world where \[desired outcome\] so that \[core human benefit\].”_
6.  **Test for resonance.**  
    Ask:
    *   Does it inspire emotion or imagination?
    *   Would your team proudly repeat it?
    *   Does it describe a future worth working for even if unreachable in full?

**Examples:**

*   **Tesla:** “To accelerate the world’s transition to sustainable energy.”
*   **Patagonia:** “A world where our business can be used to save our home planet.”
*   **Gigaton-aligned example:** “A world of profound understanding and purposeful abundance born from human–tech co-evolution.”
