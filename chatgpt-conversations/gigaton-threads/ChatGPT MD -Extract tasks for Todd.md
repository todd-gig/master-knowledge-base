## Prompt:
explain what a consent flag would be

## Response:
A **consent flag** is a **machine-readable assertion of granted authority**, not a legal checkbox and not a UX artifact. It is a **stateful control primitive** that gates what the system is allowed to do _right now_, under _which conditions_, and _with what revocation semantics_.

Below is the precise framing that matters for payload contracts.

* * *

What a Consent Flag _Is_
------------------------

A consent flag is a **verifiable signal** that:

> _An actor has explicitly authorized a specific class of actions, within defined bounds, for a defined purpose, for a defined duration._

It is **operational consent**, not philosophical consent.

* * *

What a Consent Flag Is _Not_
----------------------------

*   ❌ Not “I agree to the terms”
*   ❌ Not static or permanent
*   ❌ Not global
*   ❌ Not trust-based
*   ❌ Not human-only

If it can’t be evaluated by a machine without interpretation, it’s not a consent flag.

* * *

Core Properties of a Consent Flag
---------------------------------

Each consent flag must encode **all six dimensions** below:

1.  **Subject**
    *   Who is granting consent
*   Human, agent, organization, or node
    2.  **Scope**
    *   _Exactly_ what actions are permitted
*   Example: `read.voice.transcript`, `execute.outbound_call`, `derive.causal_model`
    3.  **Purpose**
    *   Why the action is allowed
*   Prevents scope creep and secondary use
    4.  **Duration**
    *   When consent starts and expires
*   Supports time-boxing and decay
    5.  **Constraints**
    *   Limits on volume, frequency, sensitivity, or escalation
*   Example: “no autonomous action without human review”
    6.  **Revocation Semantics**
    *   How consent is withdrawn
*   Immediate, delayed, conditional, or event-triggered

* * *

----------------------------------------

consent_flag: {

Why Consent Flags Matter in Causal Systems
------------------------------------------

In a self-regulating system:

*   **Consent is a causal boundary**
*   **Violating consent is a state transition error**
*   **Consent governs agency, not access**

This enables:

*   autonomous operation _without_ runaway behavior
*   enforceable ethical constraints _without_ hardcoding rules
*   legal defensibility _without_ constant human intervention

* * *

Consent Flags vs Permissions (Critical Distinction)
---------------------------------------------------

| Concept | Permissions | Consent Flags |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Static | Yes | No |
| Context-aware | No | Yes |
| Time-bound | Rarely | Always |
| Revocable | Often manual | Native |
| Purpose-limited | No | Yes |
