## Prompt:
is there a matterport app that works on Mac desktop for laptops instead of iPhone or ipad

## Response:
Great question — and this is one a lot of Matterport users run into.

Here’s the **short version:**  
👉 **No, Matterport does not currently offer a native capture app for Mac desktop or laptop computers.**  
The **Matterport Capture app** — the one used to connect to the camera, scan spaces, and upload models — is **iOS-only** (iPhone and iPad).

* * *

### ✅ Current Options and Workarounds

**1\. Use the iOS Capture App (official route)**

*   Requires an **iPhone or iPad (iOS 15+)**.
*   Works with: Pro2, Pro3, Axis, Ricoh Theta, Insta360, etc.
*   After capture, models sync to your Matterport Cloud, accessible from any device (including Mac).

**2\. Access, Edit, and Manage on Mac**

*   You _can_ use the **Matterport web portal** (via Safari or Chrome on macOS) to:
    *   View spaces
    *   Edit and label models
    *   Create guided tours
    *   Manage projects, folders, and collaborators
    *   Download assets (e.g., OBJ, point cloud, floor plan data)  
        ➜ [https://my.matterport.com](https://my.matterport.com)

**3\. Emulator Workaround (Not officially supported)**

*   You can run **iOS Matterport Capture** using an emulator like:
    *   **Corellium (paid, developer-grade)** – near-native iOS emulation
    *   **Xcode + Simulator** (free, advanced setup; requires Apple Developer tools)
*   However, these don’t provide **hardware connectivity** for 3D cameras, so you can’t capture — only explore the app interface.

**4\. Alternative Scanning Apps (macOS-compatible)**  
If your goal is to capture or process 3D spaces **directly from your Mac**, you could consider:

*   **Polycam for Web** (upload LiDAR or 3D photos to process online)
*   **Metareal Stage** (browser-based 3D tour builder; supports DSLR uploads)
*   **CupixWorks** (enterprise 3D capture using 360 cameras, browser managed)
*   **RealityCapture** or **Agisoft Metashape** (for photogrammetry using DSLR images on macOS)

These alternatives let you capture with other devices (e.g., 360 camera or DSLR), then process and edit fully on your Mac.

* * *

### 💡 Practical Setup (if you want desktop workflow)

If you want a **desktop-based professional capture + edit flow**, a hybrid approach works best:

1.  **Capture** with an iPhone/iPad + 360 or Pro camera.
2.  **Upload to Matterport Cloud.**
3.  **Process, edit, and export** assets on your **Mac desktop.**
4.  Optionally **integrate with Blender, Unreal, or SketchUp** for advanced editing.
