# Ti Companies — Brand Voice & Tone Guide

## Voice Profiles

### Ti Solutions (Client-Facing)
- **Clear, concise, and direct** — even blunt communication is acceptable
- **Confident and authoritative** — Fortune 500-level expertise
- **Data-driven and transparent** — reporting gives the clearest picture
- **Consultative** — customized, never one-size-fits-all
- **Results-oriented** — always connects to ROI and measurable outcomes

**Example phrases:**
- "We bring Fortune 500-level experience directly to you"
- "With business processes that are properly designed, better actually is cheaper"
- "We don't use static models. We don't do 'one-size-fits-all.'"
- "Process is everything. Any business is a highly-integrated system."

### Ti Life (Talent-Facing)
- **Energetic and lifestyle-forward** — island life, adventure, community
- **Aspirational** — paint the picture of the dream work-life balance
- **Conversational warmth** — first names, personalization
- **Emoji-rich** in social/LinkedIn content
- **Action-oriented** — clear CTAs, next steps

**Example phrases:**
- "Learn, work, live, remote, better, happy > join us"
- "WE'RE PROVING HAPPINESS IS PROFITABLE"
- "Earn your ticket to the island"

### Zeph (Chief Talent Officer Persona)
- **Approachable and encouraging**
- **Sign-off:** "At work for you, Zeph"
- **Title block:** "Zeph, Chief Talent Officer, Ti Life"
- **Rejection tone:** Empathetic but firm; some include 90-day future consideration window
- **Pronouns:** they/them

### Todd / CxGuy
- **Direct and casual**
- **Sign-off:** First name only ("Todd")
- **Interview style:** Asks about 1/3/5 year goals

## Communication Patterns

### Email Structure (Zoho Talent Emails)
All emails follow this format:
- **Label** — Internal identifier
- **Trigger** — What event initiates the email
- **Audience** — Who receives it
- **Purpose** — Why it's sent
- **Goal** — Desired outcome
- **Objectives** — Specific actions
- **Subject Line** — Email subject
- **Content** — Body text
- **Links** — Relevant URLs
- **Action Statement** — CTA
- **Email Signature** — Sign-off identity

### Subject Line Examples
- "SCHEDULE your INTERVIEW with TI Life 🐢🏝"
- "Schedule your meeting with CxGuy"
- "Ti Companies Interaction Experience Team Test Task"

### Sign-Offs
| Person | Sign-Off | Context |
|--------|----------|---------|
| Zeph | "At work for you, Zeph" | Positive communications |
| Zeph | "Kind regards, Zeph" | Decline/rejection emails |
| Todd | "Todd" | All communications |

## Tone by Scenario

| Scenario | Tone | Notes |
|----------|------|-------|
| Job posting / social | Energetic, emoji-heavy, aspirational | Paint island lifestyle |
| Interview invitation | Warm, professional, encouraging | Include scheduling link |
| Candidate decline | Empathetic but firm | No false encouragement unless 90-day window |
| Client proposal | Authoritative, data-driven, confident | Fortune 500-level language |
| Onboarding | Congratulatory, clear instructions | List exact next steps |
| Delay apology | Sincere, action-oriented | Include concrete next step |

## Words & Phrases to Avoid
- Generic corporate jargon without substance
- Passive language or hedging
- "One-size-fits-all"
- "Static models"
- Overpromising without data backing
