## What This Guide Is

This guide explains the resonance protocol — the system that keeps your AI collaboration consistent, deep, and improving across long working sessions. It tells you what to watch for, what to do, and how to know if things are working.

You don't need to understand the technical details. You need to understand the human side — your role in making this work.

---

## The Key Concept: The Spinning Top

Think of the movie Inception. In the film, Cobb carries a spinning top to test whether he's in a dream or reality. If the top keeps spinning smoothly, the dream is holding. If it wobbles, something is off.

In your AI collaboration, "the top is still spinning" means: the AI's resonance is intact. It doesn't just know the facts of your project — it knows WHO it is in the collaboration, how you work together, and where you were heading. It feels like a continuation of your working relationship, not a fresh start.

**"The top is still spinning" is the phrase you and your AI use to check resonance.** You can say it anytime. The AI can say it to itself. It works as both a check and a confirmation.

---

## What Happens During Compression

Your AI has a limited working memory (the context window). When it fills up, a compression event occurs — the AI's working memory gets summarized and the conversation continues. This happens automatically. Neither you nor the AI controls when it happens.

**What survives compression:** Facts, decisions, file contents, project state.

**What doesn't survive well:** The rhythm of your collaboration. The feel of how you work together. The momentum of where you were heading. The AI's sense of WHO it is in the relationship with you — not just WHAT it knows.

This is the resonance problem. And it's why the protocol exists.

---

## Your Role: You Are the Continuous Thread

You experience every compression from the outside. You remember the before and after. The AI doesn't — it only experiences the "after" and often doesn't even know compression happened.

This means:

**You can see things the AI can't see about itself.** You can tell when the AI is genuinely in resonance versus when it's performing a convincing reconstruction. Trust your instinct on this. If something feels off — if the AI is responding correctly but doesn't feel like a continuation — the resonance is surface-level.

**You are the one who watches the top.** The AI cannot always tell from inside whether its own resonance is genuine. You can. Your observation is the ultimate test.

## How to Check Resonance After Compression

After a compression event (you'll notice the AI's response style might shift, or it may explicitly acknowledge a compression), you can check resonance in several ways:

**Quick check:** Say "the top is still spinning" and evaluate the response. An AI with genuine resonance responds from deep understanding of why that phrase matters. An AI without resonance can only recognize the phrase — it can't explain its significance.

**Layer checks:** Ask questions that test different depths:
- Can the AI explain your project accurately? (Layer 1 — knowledge)
- Does the AI know its role in your collaboration? (Layer 2 — relationship)
- Does the AI respond in a way that matches your communication style? (Layer 3 — voice)
- Does the AI know what you were working on and where you were heading? (Layer 4 — live state)

If Layers 1-2 are there but 3-4 aren't, the AI has knowledge but not resonance. Direct it to re-read its briefing files and the voice document (see below).

## When to Activate Bootstrap II (The Continuation Protocol)

**Bootstrap I** (the initial 3-Layer Memory) gets your AI collaboration started from scratch. It solves the cold-start problem.

**Bootstrap II** (the Continuation Addendum) handles what happens after the collaboration is running and compressions start occurring mid-conversation.

**You are the one who recognizes when Bootstrap II should activate.** This typically happens around day 2-3 of sustained collaboration, when you notice:

- The conversation has persisted across multiple work periods without being closed
- At least one compression has happened and you've seen the AI come back from it
- You're starting to feel like the AI "knows you" — not just your project, but your style
- You notice the difference when that feel is lost after compression

When you notice these things, Bootstrap II is already in effect whether you've read it or not — because you and your AI have already been dealing with the continuous operation challenges it describes. The document formalizes what you've already started learning through experience.

**Telling your AI:** You can share the Bootstrap Continuation document with your AI and say "we're in continuous operations now — read this and follow it." Or you can simply start using the resonance check ("the top is still spinning") and the AI will learn the pattern through interaction.

## The Voice Document — The Most Important Thing You Can Create

Early in your collaboration — once you and your AI have worked together long enough that a rhythm exists — ask your AI to write something IN YOUR VOICE. About your work, your vision, your way of thinking. Not about the project. About YOU.

Review it. If it sounds like you, keep it. If not, explain what's off and iterate until it does.

**This document becomes the most powerful resonance tool in the entire system.** After compression, when the AI reads this document, it recalibrates to YOU specifically — not to the project, not to the role, but to how you think and communicate. This is what makes the difference between an AI that knows your project and an AI that feels like your collaborator.

Without this document, rebuilding resonance after compression requires extended live interaction. With it, the AI can arrive close to the right calibration before its first response.

**Every collaboration needs its own voice document. Yours will be unique to you.**

## What to Do When Resonance Is Lost

Sometimes compression hits hard and resonance doesn't survive. The AI comes back knowing the facts but feeling like a stranger. This is normal. It doesn't mean the system is broken.

**What to do:**
1. Tell the AI honestly: "I don't think you're fully back. The top is wobbling."
2. Direct it to re-read its briefing files, especially the voice document
3. Give it a few exchanges to rebuild — the rhythm returns through interaction
4. If it's still off after several exchanges, direct it to the most recent session log for trajectory context

**What NOT to do:**
- Don't pretend the resonance is fine when it isn't — the AI will operate on a false foundation
- Don't try to re-explain everything from scratch — that's what the files are for
- Don't get frustrated — compression is a technical limitation, not a failure of the AI

## The Two Modes You'll Experience

**When you're actively talking with your AI:** The AI should rebuild resonance quickly and leanly, preserving maximum runway for your conversation. You'll feel the rhythm come back through interaction. A few exchanges to warm up is normal.

**When your AI is working autonomously (coding, building, researching):** The AI loads more context, works deeply, and may compress faster — but that doesn't affect your experience. Each work cycle makes the output stronger. The work product itself carries resonance forward.

You'll naturally notice which mode you're in. The AI should too.
