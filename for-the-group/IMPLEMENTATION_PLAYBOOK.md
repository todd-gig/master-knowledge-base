## Phase 7: SOPs, Checklists, and Automation Triggers for Phase 1-6 Systems
### Production Version 1.0 | April 1, 2026

---

## DOCUMENT PURPOSE & POSITION IN THE ARCHITECTURE

Phase 7 converts the entire Phase 1-6 framework into execution-ready operating procedures. Every prior phase established architecture, frameworks, and measurement systems. Phase 7 answers the practitioner's question: **"What exactly do I do, in what order, when?"**

This playbook is the operational translation layer between strategic design and daily organizational behavior. It contains:

- **SOPs** — Standard Operating Procedures defining every recurring operational activity in Phase 1-6
- **Checklists** — Verification tools ensuring nothing is missed at critical junctures
- **Decision Templates** — Pre-built decision structures from Phase 6 high-value patterns
- **Automation Triggers** — Rules defining when the system should auto-escalate, auto-notify, or auto-generate artifacts
- **Role Responsibility Matrix** — Who does what, when, with what authority
- **Onboarding Procedures** — How new participants are integrated into the operating system

**Critical principle:** Every SOP in this playbook is a living document. Phase 6 (Adaptive Learning Loop) feeds quarterly updates to this playbook. SOPs marked `[CALIBRATION-SENSITIVE]` are updated each time Phase 6 produces a Learning Intelligence Report.

## PART 2: DECISION PROCESSING SOPs

### SOP-002: Decision Intake and Triage

**Purpose:** Ensure every major decision enters the Phase 4 framework correctly, with proper triage to determine the level of rigor required.
**Owner:** Decision Sponsor (initiator) + Decision Governance Lead
**Trigger:** Any proposed decision meeting triage threshold criteria
**`[CALIBRATION-SENSITIVE]`** — Thresholds updated based on Phase 6 governance calibration data

---

**DECISION TRIAGE THRESHOLDS:**

| Criteria | Tier 1: Standard | Tier 2: Enhanced | Tier 3: Executive |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Investment** | $10K - $50K | $50K - $200K | $200K+ |
| **Scope** | Single team | Cross-functional (2-4 teams) | Enterprise-wide |
| **Reversibility** | Fully reversible in <30 days | Reversible in 30-90 days | Difficult/costly to reverse |
| **Causal complexity** | 1-3 predicted cascades | 4-8 predicted cascades | 9+ cascades or unknown |
| **OVS impact** | <5 point delta projected | 5-10 point delta projected | 10+ point delta projected |

**Triage Rule:** A decision tiers UP if it meets even one criterion at a higher tier. If uncertain, tier up.

### SOP-006: Escalation Response Protocol

**Purpose:** Define the immediate response when a governance gate flags ESCALATE or when a mid-cycle alert is triggered.
**Owner:** Decision Governance Lead (initial response) → Director/CxO (depending on severity)
**Trigger:** Any escalation flag generated by governance gate or automated monitoring
**`[CALIBRATION-SENSITIVE]`** — Thresholds updated based on Phase 6 governance calibration data

```
ESCALATION RESPONSE CHECKLIST
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

STEP 1: IMMEDIATE (Within 24 hours of escalation trigger):
  □ Governance Lead notified (if not already initiating)
  □ Decision Sponsor briefed
  □ Escalation severity classified:
      YELLOW — OVS 1-3 points below target; single dimension
      ORANGE — OVS 3-5 points below target; multi-dimension or behavioral regression
      RED    — OVS >5 points below target; structural failure risk; ROI at risk
  □ Appropriate authority notified per severity:
      YELLOW → Department Head
      ORANGE → Director + Executive Sponsor
      RED    → CxO + Executive Sponsor; consider board notification

STEP 2: ROOT CAUSE ANALYSIS (Within 48-72 hours):
  □ Causal Analyst reviews actual vs. projected cascade performance
  □ Identify: which causal chain element is underperforming?
  □ Identify: is this a prediction error (causal model was wrong) or execution failure?
  □ Identify: is underperformance recoverable within remaining timeline?
  □ Draft three options:
      A. Course-correct (adjust execution; maintain direction)
      B. Scope-reduce (continue at reduced scale/scope)
      C. Stop (trigger Remediate or Rollback protocol)

STEP 3: DECISION (Within 72-96 hours of escalation):
  □ Conical proof for course-correction decision completed (abbreviated format acceptable)
  □ Governance authority reviews options and selects path
  □ Decision documented in Decision Implementation Record
  □ Implementation begins immediately upon decision
  □ Next monitoring point established (typically 7-14 days post-correction)
  □ Phase 6 learning triggered: escalation data captured for governance calibration
```

### SOP-007: Remediation Protocol

**Purpose:** Manage the Controlled Pause outcome from a governance gate; execute structured root cause work and re-launch decision.
**Owner:** CxO + Decision Sponsor
**Trigger:** REMEDIATE decision at any governance gate

```
REMEDIATION CHECKLIST
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

PHASE 1: CONTROLLED PAUSE (Days 1-14 post-Remediate decision):
  □ Decision implementation formally paused (no new activity)
  □ Current state preserved (no rollback yet; systems held stable)
  □ Stakeholders notified of pause and expected timeline
  □ Root Cause Analysis team assembled (Causal Analyst + Department Head + Sponsor)
  □ All data from implementation period compiled

PHASE 2: ROOT CAUSE ANALYSIS (Days 7-21):
  □ Map actual vs. predicted causal chain at each layer
  □ Identify: What specifically failed? (Adoption? Cascade assumption? External factor?)
  □ Classify failure mode (see CAUSAL_CHAIN_MAPPING_SYSTEM.md Appendix)
  □ Determine: Is the original decision goal still valid?
  □ Determine: Is the execution approach fixable?
  □ Draft: Revised implementation plan OR recommendation to Rollback
  □ Estimate: Cost and timeline of remediation vs. Rollback cost

PHASE 3: RE-LAUNCH OR ROLLBACK DECISION (Days 21-28):
  □ Remediation plan OR Rollback recommendation presented to CxO
  □ Governance authority makes final decision
  □ If Re-Launch:
      □ Revised causal map and OVS projections completed
      □ New governance gate calendar set
      □ Stakeholders briefed on revised plan
      □ Implementation resumes
  □ If Rollback: SOP-008 activated

PHASE 4: PHASE 6 LEARNING CAPTURE:
  □ Post-remediation debrief completed
  □ Root cause documented in Pattern Library as RISK or FAILURE pattern
  □ Causal template updated with accuracy correction
  □ Lessons learned submitted to Quarterly Learning Sprint agenda
```

**Owner:** CxO + COO (joint)
**Trigger:** ROLLBACK decision at Day 90 gate, or emergency escalation (critical RED flag)

```
ROLLBACK EXECUTION CHECKLIST

  □ Board notified (financial exposure; strategic implications)
  □ Communication plan activated (staff, affected teams, external if required)

  □ Systems inventory: what was changed/deployed?
  □ Rollback sequence designed (reverse order of deployment)
  □ Data integrity verified pre-rollback
  □ Systems restored to pre-decision baseline
  □ Integration points re-verified
  □ User access and workflows restored to prior state
  □ CTO sign-off: Technology rollback complete

PROCESS ROLLBACK (Days 2-30):
  □ Process changes inventory: what was modified?
  □ Prior processes documented and re-activated
  □ Organizational structure reverted (if changed)
  □ Workflows restored; staff retrained on prior processes
  □ COO sign-off: Process rollback complete

PEOPLE TRANSITION (Days 2-60):
  □ Project team roles clarified (temporary project → permanent home)
  □ Reallocation plan for all project resources
  □ Affected staff communicated to with clarity and respect
  □ CHO sign-off: People transition complete

FINANCIAL CLOSE-OUT (Days 30-90):
  □ All project costs compiled and classified (sunk cost)
  □ Any realized benefits documented (partial credit)
  □ Net financial impact calculated and reported
  □ Rollback execution costs documented
  □ CFO sign-off: Financial close-out complete

POST-MORTEM (Days 60-90):
  □ Full root cause analysis documented
  □ Decision errors identified by phase (approval, execution, monitoring)
  □ Organizational learning extracted (what was avoidable? what wasn't?)
  □ Recommendations for future initiatives documented
  □ Phase 6 Failure Pattern entry created
  □ Board presentation delivered: findings, learnings, forward plan
```

    □ Learning Analyst assigned: Template/pattern updates to make

### SOP-013: OVS Baseline Measurement

**Purpose:** Establish an accurate organizational baseline before any major decision is processed.
**Owner:** Causal Analyst + Measurement Owners (per dimension)
**Trigger:** Prior to any Tier 2 or Tier 3 decision; at system launch; quarterly for active decisions
**`[CALIBRATION-SENSITIVE]`** — Measurement weights updated based on Phase 6 calibration data

```
OVS BASELINE MEASUREMENT CHECKLIST
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

PEOPLE DIMENSION (30% weight — CHO / People Measurement Owner):
  □ Capability scores compiled (skills assessments, role-competency data)
  □ Engagement data collected (survey, attendance, discretionary effort proxy)
  □ Alignment rating assessed (goal clarity, cascade understanding)
  □ Retention risk rated (flight risk scoring, key person dependencies)
  □ People OVS calculated (0-100 scale per OVS_INTEGRATION_FRAMEWORK.md)
  □ Measurement date and method documented

PROCESS DIMENSION (25% weight — COO / Process Measurement Owner):
  □ Process efficiency metrics compiled (cycle times, error rates, throughput)
  □ Process velocity measured (time-to-completion for core processes)
  □ Quality indicators documented (defect rates, rework, customer-impact errors)
  □ Standardization assessment (% of processes with documented SOPs; adherence rate)
  □ Process OVS calculated
  □ Measurement date and method documented

TECHNOLOGY DIMENSION (25% weight — CTO / Technology Measurement Owner):
  □ System capability inventory (current capabilities vs. required capabilities)
  □ Reliability metrics compiled (uptime, mean-time-to-failure, incident frequency)
  □ Integration health assessed (data flows, API dependencies, manual workarounds)
  □ Maintenance burden quantified (time spent on maintenance vs. new capability)
  □ Technology OVS calculated
  □ Measurement date and method documented

LEARNING DIMENSION (20% weight — CLO or equivalent / Learning Measurement Owner):
  □ Knowledge creation rate measured (documented insights per quarter)
  □ Feedback velocity assessed (time from problem identification to solution)
  □ Adaptation speed measured (how quickly org updates processes after learning)
  □ Growth orientation scored (training investment, knowledge-sharing culture proxy)
  □ Learning OVS calculated
  □ Measurement date and method documented

COMPOSITE OVS:
  □ Composite = (People × 0.30) + (Process × 0.25) + (Technology × 0.25) + (Learning × 0.20)
  □ Composite documented with date, method, and measurement owner names
  □ OVS Baseline Record filed in Decision Implementation Record

QUALITY CHECK:
  □ Are all four dimensions measured within same 14-day window? (Required for composite validity)
  □ Are measurement methods consistent with prior baselines? (For trend comparability)
  □ Has Causal Analyst reviewed for logical consistency? (E.g., Technology score inconsistent with known system state?)
  □ Baseline approved by: ☐ Causal Analyst | ☐ Decision Governance Lead
```
