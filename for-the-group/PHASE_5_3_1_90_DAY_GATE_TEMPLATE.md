## SECTION 1: GATE OBJECTIVE & AUTHORITY FRAMEWORK

### 1.1 Gate Purpose & Strategic Role

The 90-day governance gate represents the final validation checkpoint before organizational integration and sustained operational deployment. Unlike Days 30 and 60 which assess layer-specific execution and immediate secondary effects, the Day 90 gate performs **complete outcome validation across all cumulative effects** and determines whether the initiative transitions from controlled, time-bound deployment to permanent organizational capability.

**Strategic Questions Answered at Day 90:**

1. Have all projected compound effects (Layer 1-3) materialized as predicted by the OVS Impact Projection Framework?
2. Has behavioral alignment sustained above 75% across all populations (individuals, managers, teams, cross-functional groups)?
3. Have structural adaptations been institutionalized, or do they remain dependent on project-level governance?
4. Has end-to-end ROI matched or exceeded Phase 4 approved estimates?
5. Is the organization ready for sustained operational ownership, or does continued monitoring/support remain necessary?

### 1.2 Decision Authority & Governance Structure

| Authority Level | Role | Approval Rights | Escalation Threshold |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Primary Authority** | Chief Operating Officer + Executive Sponsor (joint) | Finalize, Continue, Remediate, Rollback | All decisions; Board notification if Rollback |
| **Secondary Authority** | CFO (financial validation) | Financial assumptions review; investment carry-forward approval | >10% variance from approved ROI; restructured investment requirements |
| **Advisory Authority** | Department Head (implementation owner) | Implementation reality check; risk mitigation recommendations | Unresolved structural dependencies; resource constraints |
| **Escalation Authority** | Board Finance Committee (if Rollback) | Strategic review; organizational impact assessment | Business continuity implications; reputational risk |

**Decision Timeline:** Day 87-90, with assessment period Days 75-87

### 3.1 Behavioral Alignment Sustainability (Layer 2-3 Sustained)

**Definition:** Continued voluntary adoption and meaningful engagement with new systems, processes, and organizational structures across all population segments, without project-driven reinforcement.

**Measurement Cadence:** Weekly Days 75-87, daily aggregation Days 88-90

**Threshold Standards:**

| Population | Target | PASS Threshold | AT RISK Range | FAIL Threshold | Escalation |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Overall Population | >75% | ≥75% | 70-74% | <70% | Level 2 (48h Department Head) |
| Managers (n2+) | >80% | ≥80% | 75-79% | <75% | Level 2 (48h Department Head) |
| Individual Contributors | >70% | ≥70% | 65-69% | <65% | Level 1 (24h Team Lead) |
| Cross-Functional Teams | >75% | ≥75% | 70-74% | <70% | Level 2 (48h Department Head) |

**Assessment Components:**

**3.1a Voluntary Adoption Without Reinforcement**
- % users actively using systems in Day 75-87 period without project-driven incentives
- % users completing advanced workflows without supervisor prompting
- % power users maintaining >80% utilization (vs. minimum compliance)
- Metric: DAU/MAU ratio for optional features

**Acceptance Criteria:** ≥75% overall voluntary adoption

**3.1b Behavioral Persistence Across Populations**
- Manager effectiveness in coaching adoption (360 feedback)
- Individual contributor initiative in problem-solving with new tools
- Cross-functional team collaboration in integrated workflows
- Peer-to-peer knowledge transfer without formal training

**Acceptance Criteria:** >75% overall, no population <65%

**3.1c Satisfaction & Advocacy**
- NPS or satisfaction score for new systems (target: >7/10)
- % users who would recommend system to peer in same role
- Anecdotal feedback themes (thematic analysis of Day 75-87 survey responses)
- Reduction in "workaround" discovery (fewer alternative processes emerging)

**Acceptance Criteria:** ≥70% satisfaction; <20% workaround adoption
