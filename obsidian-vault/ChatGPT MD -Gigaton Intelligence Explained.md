## Prompt:
what is a first class citizen

## Response:
**Definition (straight):**  
A _first-class citizen_ is any entity that is treated as a **native, fully supported, and autonomous participant** in a system—without needing wrappers, exceptions, or special handling.

If something is first-class, the system is _designed around it_, not merely tolerant of it.

* * *

Canonical meaning (software & systems)
--------------------------------------

An entity is a first-class citizen if it can:

*   **Exist independently** (not embedded or implied)
*   **Be created, stored, passed, and referenced directly**
*   **Own state**
*   **Participate in all core operations**
*   **Have equal standing to other core entities**

Classic example:

*   In many languages, _functions_ are first-class citizens because they can be passed as arguments, returned from other functions, and stored in variables.

Non-example:

*   Comments, macros, or middleware hooks — they exist, but they have no standing.

* * *

Why this matters (the real implication)
---------------------------------------

First-class status determines:

*   **What the system optimizes for**
*   **What survives refactors**
*   **What compounds value over time**
*   **What is governable vs. duct-taped**

Anything not first-class:

*   Becomes brittle
*   Accumulates technical debt
*   Requires enforcement instead of guarantees

* * *

First-class vs second-class (practical contrast)
------------------------------------------------

| Aspect | First-Class | Second-Class |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Representation | Native | Indirect |
| State | Persistent | Ephemeral |
| Rights | Intrinsic | Enforced externally |
| Access | Direct | Mediated |
| Evolution | Designed for | Worked around |

In infrastructure terms

Bottom line

A _first-class citizen_ is something the system **cannot ignore without breaking its core logic**.

If removing it breaks _policy_, it’s second-class.  
If removing it breaks the _system_, it’s first-class.

Gigaton’s differentiation is that it elevates **humans, agents, data, and value flows** to first-class status—so governance, intelligence, and economics scale by design rather than enforcement.
