## Prompt:
what is society of minds

## Response:
_Society of Mind_ is a theory of intelligence developed by Marvin Minsky, one of the founders of artificial intelligence. It proposes that the human mind is **not a single, unified entity**, but rather a **collection (a “society”) of many smaller, simpler agents**—each responsible for specific functions or thought patterns—that work together to produce complex behavior and consciousness.

Key concepts:

*   **Agents:** Small, specialized processes that do simple things (e.g., recognizing faces, recalling memories, evaluating actions).
*   **Hierarchies and Networks:** These agents are organized hierarchically and in networks, where higher-level agents coordinate lower-level ones.
*   **Emergent Intelligence:** Intelligence arises not from a single algorithm, but from the interactions and cooperation between these many agents.
*   **No Central Controller:** There’s no “boss” in the mind; coordination happens through distributed processes and feedback loops.

In modern AI terms, it anticipates **multi-agent systems** and **modular architectures**—where intelligence is achieved by many subsystems collaborating dynamically rather than one monolithic brain. It’s foundational for thinking about **emergent behavior, distributed cognition, and artificial general intelligence (AGI)**.
