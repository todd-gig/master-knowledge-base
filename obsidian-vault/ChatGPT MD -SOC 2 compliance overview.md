## Response:
SOC 2 compliance (often mistyped as _sock2_) is a cybersecurity and data protection standard developed by the **American Institute of CPAs (AICPA)**.

It’s essentially an auditing framework that evaluates how well a company manages **customer data** in the context of security, availability, processing integrity, confidentiality, and privacy.

Here’s the breakdown:

### 🔑 What SOC 2 Is

*   **SOC** = _System and Organization Controls_.
*   **SOC 2** = specifically designed for **service providers** that store or process customer data in the cloud or via software platforms (think SaaS, IT service firms, cloud vendors, fintechs, etc.).

### 🧩 The Core Trust Service Principles (TSPs)

SOC 2 audits measure an organization against one or more of these five principles:

1.  **Security** – Systems are protected against unauthorized access (firewalls, MFA, intrusion detection, etc.).
2.  **Availability** – Systems are available and reliable as agreed (uptime, monitoring, disaster recovery).
3.  **Processing Integrity** – Data is processed accurately, completely, and in a timely manner.
4.  **Confidentiality** – Sensitive information is protected from unauthorized disclosure.
5.  **Privacy** – Personal information is collected, used, retained, and disclosed appropriately.

### 📑 The Types of SOC 2 Reports

*   **SOC 2 Type I** – Point-in-time audit. Checks if the company has the right controls in place _on a specific date_.
*   **SOC 2 Type II** – More rigorous. Evaluates how effective those controls are _over a period of time_ (usually 6–12 months).  
    👉 Most enterprise clients will insist on **Type II** before signing contracts.

### 🎯 Why It Matters

*   Demonstrates **trust and accountability** in handling sensitive customer data.
*   Often a **requirement for doing business** in industries like SaaS, fintech, healthtech, and B2B services.
*   Helps reduce legal, reputational, and operational risk.
*   Boosts competitiveness: “We are SOC 2 compliant” is a strong sales credential.
