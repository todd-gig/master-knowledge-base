## Article 1 – Name and Purpose

1.1 **Name**  
The name of this entity is **Attractor Dynamix** (the “Institution”).

1.2 **Purpose**  
Attractor Dynamix exists to steward and protect a shared memory‑model intellectual property foundation and to enable its co‑founders to use and commercialize that foundation through their own independent companies. The Institution is governed by the following operating ethos: do not harm each other, help each other when possible, and learn from each other through transparent collaboration.

---

## Article 2 – Core Intellectual Property

2.1 **Core IP Definition**  
“Core IP” means the geometric / causal memory model and related inventions as described in the patent application(s) jointly designated by the four co‑founders for protection under this Constitution, together with any continuations, divisionals, reissues, or foreign equivalents.

2.2 **Patent Co‑Ownership**  
The Core IP patent(s) are owned in equal undivided shares (25% each) by the four founding members (the “Founders”), whether held directly in their personal names or assigned to Attractor Dynamix in equal beneficial interests as agreed in the separate patent filing and assignment documents.

2.3 **Code and Non‑Core IP**  
(a) Any existing codebase already written by a particular Founder remains that Founder’s sole property and is not automatically included in Core IP.  
(b) Each Founder retains full ownership of their own company’s products, brands, and non‑Core IP. Sharing of such non‑Core IP requires a separate written agreement between the relevant parties.

---

## Article 3 – Licenses and Use Rights

3.1 **Use in Each Founder’s Company**  
Each Founder and each Founder‑controlled company receives a non‑exclusive, worldwide, royalty‑free, perpetual license to use the Core IP in their own products and services.

3.2 **No Cross‑Ownership of Businesses**  
Nothing in this Constitution gives any Founder or Attractor Dynamix any ownership, profit share, or governance rights in any other Founder’s company. The only shared asset governed here is the Core IP.

3.3 **Third‑Party Licensing (Non‑Exclusive)**  
Any Founder may propose a non‑exclusive third‑party license of the Core IP. Approval requires at least three of four Founders. Net revenue from any such Core‑IP license is split equally (25% each) among the Founders, unless otherwise agreed in writing for a specific deal.

3.4 **Exclusive Licenses and Sale**  
No exclusive license, lien, or sale of the Core IP (in whole or in part) may be granted without unanimous written consent of all Founders.

---

## Article 4 – Cost Sharing and Contributions

4.1 **Patent and Legal Costs**  
All out‑of‑pocket costs for prosecution, maintenance, and defense of the Core IP patent(s) (including official fees and agreed legal fees) are shared equally among the Founders (25% each).

4.2 **Non‑Payment**  
If one or more Founders fail to pay their share when due, the others may advance the missing amount. The non‑paying Founder’s ownership share in the Core IP is not reduced solely by non‑payment, but the non‑paying Founder must fully reimburse any advanced amounts (with simple interest at an agreed reasonable rate) before receiving any distributions of Core‑IP‑related license revenue or any buyout proceeds.

4.3 **Initial Capital**  
The Founders may agree separately on modest initial capital contributions to cover formation and first‑filing costs; such contributions are treated as loans or equity contributions to Attractor Dynamix as determined in writing at the time they are made.

---

## Article 5 – Decision‑Making and Speed

5.1 **Decision Scope**  
This minimal Constitution is intentionally narrow. It governs only: (a) Core IP ownership and licensing; (b) cost sharing; (c) basic dispute resolution; and (d) triggers for adopting a fuller governance structure.

5.2 **Quorum and Voting**  
For decisions within the scope of this Constitution, quorum is any three of the four Founders participating (in person or by electronic communication). Decisions are made by at least three of four Founders, unless this document explicitly requires unanimity.

5.3 **Asynchronous Voting**  
For non‑urgent matters, any Founder may circulate a written proposal (e.g., by email or collaboration tool). Each Founder has 72 hours to vote “approve”, “reject”, or “abstain”. If at least three of four approve within the window, the decision passes. Silence is counted as abstention.

5.4 **Emergency Authority**  
Any Founder may unilaterally authorize urgent expenditures or actions necessary to prevent loss or lapse of the Core IP (for example, payment of an imminent patent office fee) up to an agreed cap (to be specified in a separate short schedule). The acting Founder must notify the others as soon as reasonably practical, and such costs are shared under Article 4.

---

## Article 6 – Disputes and Exit

6.1 **Dispute Resolution**  
If a dispute arises among the Founders relating to this Constitution, they will: (a) talk in good faith for at least 14 days; (b) if unresolved, engage a mutually acceptable mediator; and (c) if still unresolved, submit the issue to confidential binding arbitration under the rules of a reputable provider (e.g., AAA or JAMS) in a mutually agreed venue. The arbitrator may award costs and fees; by default, the losing party pays reasonable arbitration costs.

6.2 **Founder Exit**  
Any Founder may choose to exit this arrangement by giving at least 90 days’ written notice. The remaining Founders have a right of first refusal to purchase the departing Founder’s Core‑IP share on mutually agreed terms or, failing agreement, at a price determined by independent appraisal. If no Founder buys the share, the exiting Founder retains their share but remains bound by this Constitution to the extent permitted by law.

6.3 **No Forced Development**  
No Founder is obligated under this minimal Constitution to provide development, implementation, sales, or support work for any other Founder’s company. Any such work must be separately agreed, with defined scope and compensation.

---

## Article 7 – Upgrade Trigger and Evolution

7.1 **Living Document**  
This Constitution is a living document. The Founders intend to keep it under review and to strengthen its protections as the Core IP and related businesses mature.

7.2 **Scheduled Review**  
The Founders will review this Constitution at least once every 12 months.

7.3 **Upgrade Triggers**  
If any of the following occur, the Founders agree to negotiate in good faith toward a fuller governance structure for Attractor Dynamix (a more complete constitution and accompanying operating agreement) and to adopt that structure within 90 days, unless unanimously extended:

- (a) The Core IP patent is granted in any jurisdiction; or  
- (b) Any Founder’s product or service materially based on the Core IP exceeds a mutually agreed annual revenue threshold; or  
- (c) A third party makes a serious written proposal to license or acquire rights in the Core IP; or  
- (d) Any Founder proposes admitting a fifth co‑owner, investor, or institutional partner into Attractor Dynamix; or  
- (e) Any dispute about Core IP or this Constitution remains unresolved for more than 60 days after mediation is initiated.

7.4 **Amendments**  
Amendments to this minimal Constitution require at least three of four Founders, provided that any amendment that would change ownership percentages in the Core IP or allow exclusive licensing or sale without unanimity must be unanimously approved.

---

## Article 8 – Governing Law

8.1 **Governing Law and Venue**  
The Founders will specify governing law and arbitration venue in the final signed version of this Constitution, generally aligning with the jurisdiction of Attractor Dynamix’s legal formation.
